01Overview

Xenia turns every post on X (Twitter) into a tippable on-chain action. A Chrome extension injects a Tip button into the feed and onto profiles; clicking it sends value on Somnia Network that either lands instantly in the recipient's wallet or is held in an on-chain escrow until the recipient signs up — at which point they claim it with one click.

The entire stack — Solidity contracts, a Node backend, a React dashboard, and a browser extension — is built specifically around Somnia's strengths. Sub-second finality and sub-cent gas make per-post micro-tipping economically viable for the first time, turning a slow, fee-heavy chore into a single in-feed gesture.

In one sentence

Tip anyone on X by their handle, instantly and on-chain — even if they have never touched crypto.

02The problem

Tipping creators on social media is broken in two directions.

Web2 tipping

  • Both sides must create accounts
  • Platforms take 2–30% in fees
  • Payouts take days
  • Locked to one walled garden

Web3 tipping

  • Recipient needs a wallet on the right chain
  • Gas frequently costs more than the tip
  • No way to tip an off-chain identity
  • Confirmation latency kills the gesture

The net result: tipping is not a fluid, in-feed gesture. It is a friction-filled chore that almost no one actually does.

03The solution

Xenia removes both walls.

  • Tip anyone, even non-crypto users. Tips to an unregistered handle are held in an on-chain escrow keyed by the handle. The recipient signs up once, the backend binds their wallet on-chain, and the funds are theirs.
  • Tipping is a single click inside X. The extension injects a Tip button into every tweet and onto profiles, and auto-adds Somnia to the wallet so users never configure a network.
  • Two modes, one contract. A direct tip signed by the user's wallet, and a Twitter-command tip executed by an AI bot from a pre-funded, delegated balance.
  • Handle-first identity. Every flow — tip, claim, registration — is keyed by the lowercased X handle, the only identifier a sender knows at tip time.

04How it works

Mode A — direct tip (extension / web)

The user's own wallet signs Escrow.tip(handle). If the recipient is registered, STT transfers directly; otherwise it is escrowed under the handle.

Mode B — Twitter command

The user pre-deposits and authorizes the bot. They tweet a command; the bot parses intent with Gemini and calls tipOnBehalf — it can only forward funds, never withdraw.

Claim

An escrowed recipient signs up, the backend binds their handle to their wallet on-chain (immutable), and they pull all pending tips.

05Architecture

One escrow contract on Somnia, reached from four surfaces through a single backend.

The user's wallet signs every value-moving call (tip, claim, deposit, authorize). The backend wallet is the contract owner and only signs registerWallet and registerScreenshot. The bot signs tipOnBehalf from delegated deposits and can never withdraw.

08Security

  • Reentrancy guard on every value-moving external call.
  • Fee accounting separated from user funds — fee withdrawals can never touch balances.
  • Immutable wallet binding — a handle can be bound to a wallet exactly once.
  • Scoped bot delegation — tipOnBehalf can only forward to the resolved recipient, never to an arbitrary address, and never withdraw.
  • 90-day sender refund safety net for tips that are never claimed.
  • Two-step ownership transfer on both contracts; platform fee capped at 5%.
  • No custody of keys — Privy embedded wallets stay client-side; the backend never holds user private keys.
